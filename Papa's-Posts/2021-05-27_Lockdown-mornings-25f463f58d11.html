Lockdown mornings

In Banglore, in the apartment complex where I stay, lockdown brings life to standstill, every time it has been imposed(to contain corona waves). The options to keep fit seem limited but a second look reveals many possibilities.. cycling, walking, yoga, dumbbells, jogging, badminton, breathing exercises, freehand exercises and more. Lack of physical activities makes the day too dull. And you further get demotivated. This becomes a vicious cycle.

However breaking that pattern is easy. Just go for 2 consecutive days of early morning walks and the third day onwards, you look forward to it. Going for a morning walk anytime between 6AM and 8.30AM is super fun. The morning breeze, the chirping birds, the morning sun, the entire atmosphere feels heavenly. The natural effects take over and the craving for the morning bliss becomes a self sustaining cycle. Go naturally. Listen to the body. For me, the internal demand for a long walk or a jog automatically kicks in and it feels super having done that. Mind and the entire being feels light and blissful after letting out some sweat.

And after that, a cup of tea in the balcony, enjoying the morning sounds is blissful. The morning seems endless and wonderful. Naturally at some point the mind likes to start the workday and I go about starting off other activities. The day becomes much more enjoyable and rewarding.

However there are days when I skip some of the parts, don’t do any of these at all or go for some others. It’s not about being in a robotic routine. Rather listen to the body, propose and try new activities and go with the natural flow. The key is to step out of the home and discover the morning.
