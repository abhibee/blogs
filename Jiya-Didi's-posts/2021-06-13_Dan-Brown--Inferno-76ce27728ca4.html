Dan Brown: Inferno

Book review

Age rating: 13+

Author: Dan Brown

Year of publishing: 2013

Robert Langdon, a clever professor tries to stop a mad scientist who is bent on destroying the world. Robert Langdon wakes up one day in a hospital with no idea how he ended up there. Before he could process this some people barge into his room and starts shooting at him. After escaping with Sienna Brooks, a nurse at the hospital, All he could see was a recurring vision of a women with silver hair saying “seek and you shall find” he must find it before the assasin kills him. A well knitted mystery which is intertwined with Dante Alighieri’s Inferno is filled with surprises. The fate of the world lies in Langdon's hand. Will he be able to save it?

The book is very descriptive and transports the reader to another world. The mystery is so well narrated that it is really hard to put the book down. Over all I will give the book a four and half part as some of the parts are difficult to understand which the reader can only understand after reading it twice or thrice.

By Sukriti Nath on June 13, 2021.
