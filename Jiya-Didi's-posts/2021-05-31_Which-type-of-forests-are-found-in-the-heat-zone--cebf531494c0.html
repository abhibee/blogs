Which type of forests are found in the heat zone?

Broadly, there are three major forest zones that are separated according to their distance from the equator. These are:​

  • the tropical,​
  • The temperate,​
  • The boreal forests (taiga)​

There are also more specific types of forests within these larger regions.​

Tropical (torrid zone)

  • Tropical rain forests grow around the equator in South America, Africa, and Southeast Asia.​
  • The temperature remains around 27°C all year round. As the name suggests, it receives lot of rain, Most tropical forests receive at least 200 cm of rain in a year. ​
  • Broadleaf trees, mosses, ferns, palms, and orchids thrive in rain forests. The trees grow densely together and the branches and leaves block most of the light from penetrating to the understory.​

Temperate (Temperate zone) ​

  • Temperate forests occur in the next latitude ring, in North America, northeastern Asia, and Europe.​
  • generally, the temperature ranges from -30 to 30°C.The forests receive 75–150 cm (30–60 in) of precipitation per year.​
  • Common tree species are oak, beech, maple, elm, birch, willow, and hickory trees.​

Boreal (frigid zone)

  • Boreal forests, also called taiga, are found between 50 and 60 degree of latitude in the sub-Arctic zone. This area contains Siberia, Scandinavia, Alaska, and Canada.​
  • Temperatures range from -40 to 20°C. Precipitation is usually delivered as snow because of cold, 40–100 cm of snowfall is received each year.​
  • Evergreen conifers with needle leaves that can stand the cold, like pine, fir, and spruce trees, live here.​
