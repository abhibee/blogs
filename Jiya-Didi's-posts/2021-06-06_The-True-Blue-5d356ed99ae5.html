Blue, why is it so rare?

It is said that 600 million years ago nobody had eyes. Therefore color didnt matter much. Later when colors started becoming prominent the colors of the nature started tricking humans as well. Many animals, such as the blue morpho butterfly appear blue, but, they are actually not considered true blue. Only about 1% of the world’s animal are considered to have the true blue.

Why are there so less animals with “true blue”?

Many of the pigments in an animals body comes from their food. Therefore, as foods do not contain the so called “true blue” pigment, these animals are not classified as true blue.

Others, deceive us. how? they have a way to trick our eyes using the light, surroundings and physical structures. For example, the blue morpho butterflies’ wings have ridges that causes the light to bend in a way that it only shows blue. If you are lucky enough to see one, you will notice that it looks different from every angle.

So who is true blue?

So far, only one such animal is found to have the true blue pigment naturally-

The Obrina Olivewing.

No matter from which angle you see it it will always be blue.
