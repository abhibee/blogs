Television

gripping lives?

Television has created a spell which is almost unshakable and is gripping the lives of young children. Television was initially made as a medium for sharing news to the citizens but by time, informative machine turned into a nightmare for kids. Nowadays parents let their children watch television till long hours or until they finish their work peacefully, but little do they realise how it affects the vulnerable brain of a child. Once they start watching it, they stop using their minds and just stare at the screen. When a child watches tv he or she is fed something from someone else’s mind which doesn’t give them a chance to use their own. Television not only wastes time of children, but also is a major cause in rise of global warming. Many children before knowing how to read, know how to operate the television. They are getting so gripped in the television’s trap that they knowingly create nuisance so that their parents switch on the television. Nowadays, television has so many shows which are senseless and influences the younger generation. Some children even hurt themselves while imitating characters they see on television. Not only does children need to be encouraged to not watch tv but also to read books, which unleashes the creative imagination of a child. Instead of staring at the television children can play the numerous board games which are available. Not only does that sharpens the mind but also decreases the chances of getting spectacles. During contemporary times not only is television taking control over people’s life but even smartphones. Smartphones is like the television of modern times. It has innumerous positive points if used wisely but if it is not it can be even more harmful then television. Especially when parents expose their kids to smartphones at a young age. They get hooked to it which not only for harmful for the brain and eyes but also for the neck of the child. Many people because of their posture are troubled all their life. Both, television and smartphones have many bad qualities but if used wisely it can be very useful. Thus, there should be a prescribed age
