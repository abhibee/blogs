Cyclones

In view of the recent events of the cyclone Tauktae and the upcoming cyclone Yaas in this blog we will discuss how a cyclone is formed, it’s effects and how to keep safe.

Formation:

Cyclones are formed when saturated (moisture laden) air above the ocean rises skyward. When the air rises up and away from the ocean, it creates an area of low pressure below. It causes the air from adjacent areas to move towards the low-pressure area. This air again gets heated and rises again forming a cycle. As the warmed, moist air rises and cools the water in the air forms clouds. The whole system of clouds and wind spins and grows, fed by the ocean’s heat and water evaporating from the ocean surface. As the storm system rotates faster and faster, an eye forms in the centre. It is very calm and clear in the eye, with very low air pressure. Higher pressure air from above flows down into the eye.

Effects of cyclones:

Most of the times cyclone have serious effects like:

· Cyclones causes mass destruction, due to the strong winds many trees are uprooted and destroyed. Cyclones even effect animals as they might get electrocuted by some wire which break due to the winds or the rain.

· Cyclones bring thunder and lightning with them, though there is a really small chance getting struck and electrocuted by lightning, there are many people who get struck due to lack of information on precautions from it.

· Cyclones also destroy homes, as, in villages some people’s houses are not that secured and are easily torn apart due to the strong winds.

How to keep safe:

· Avoid going near trees and electricity poles as they might fall.

· Avoid touching any electrical appliances if you are wet

· During a thunder storm never be in an empty field and if you are don’t take shelter from tall trees, and stay low

· If you live in the coastal area, always keep the radio active, to get essential information about the cyclone and when to start moving towards the higher ground.
