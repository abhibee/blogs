Tribes

India is blessed with a rich diversity, this includes the numerous tribes that India has, some of these tribes make beautiful handlooms but are not that famous so in this blog I will like to tell about some of them.

There are many tribes in India, with their own cultures. Many tribes make intricate handlooms, tribal people have a lot of knowledge in traditional weaving and handlooms. Cotton, Eri Silk and Wool are common yarn used by tribal people in North India. Different tribal communities use different kind of dresses, differing in their colour and size. Their dresses are designed keeping in view their necessity and their surroundings. Some tribes buy handlooms from other tribes, instead of making it themselves. The Government of India has initiated many programs for the development of handlooms in Tribes.

Bodo Tribe

Bodo Tribe of Assam is said to have introduced silkworm rearing in the North Eastern parts of India. Women weave and wear their own Dokhnas, the traditional dress covering the chest to the ankle worn with shawls. The traditional colour is yellow usually in combination with green and parrot green. Aronai is a small Scarf, used both by Men and Women. In ancient period Boro warriors used Aronai as a belt in the battle field. Gamsha was used by Boro mento cover the portion from waist to knee by tying it in the waist.

Kuki Tribe

Kuki tribe mainly produces dwarf cotton and spun yarns. Kuki men wear colorful Sangkhol, a jacketand a ‘Pheichawm’ which is a short dhoti. Sometimes a wrap is used. They also wear Tuhpah or also known as a headcover. Women wear anih-san underneath a ponve or a wraparound. The dress is worn from above the chest. Jewelry include earrings, and necklaces.

Adi Tribe

Adi tribe is found in Arunachal Pradesh. Rice cultivation is popularly practiced. The dress worn by both men and women is made by the tribe. They also wear helmets made of cane, bear or deer skin, depending on the region. multiple necklaces and earrings are worn by women.
