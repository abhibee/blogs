History of turbans

It is found that turbans were in use even before the advent of some religions. Therefore, the origin of the turban cannot be ascribed to religious reasons alone. It is also mentioned the Old Testament and the Vedic Literature of India. These were originally worn by royalty and spiritual leaders and used to commute power, often adorned with jewels and accessories to display wealth and grandeur. The turban had been important in many cultures and religions . An exchange of turbans signifies a long-distance relationship and forges relationships between families, while presenting someone with a turban is a great token of esteem. Thus, the turban is an important part of all ceremonies from birth to death. The Sikhs wear the turban and call it dastaar, while the Muslim refer to it as the Kalansuwa.

The Patka

Sikh patka is a bandana headgear normally used when entering a place of worship such as a Gurudwara. The sikh patka is used by men and women as fashion wear to cover long or short hair. Its a type of scarf, a garment worn around the head or neck or shoulders for warmth and decoration.

The Dumalla

The dumalla is a type of turban worn by sikhs. This turban is worn mainly by sikhs who are initiated to the khalsa. The word dumalla means “du” meaning two and “malla” meaning cloth This is because there will usually be one fabric to form the base of the turban and a second to form the turban itself. there are many types of dumalla, in many types of size and colour.
