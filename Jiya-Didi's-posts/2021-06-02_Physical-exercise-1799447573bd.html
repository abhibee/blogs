Some facts about exercise

Is physical exercise a part of hygiene?

While hearing the word “hygiene” you immediately think of washing your hands, the term actually refers to any practice that provides a sense of health and well-being. As far as total-body hygiene goes, exercise is one of the best things you can do to stay healthy.

What happens inside your body when you exercise?

Regular physical activity can improve your muscle strength and boost your endurance. Exercise delivers oxygen and nutrients to your tissues and helps your cardiovascular system work more efficiently. And when your heart and lung health improve, you have more energy to tackle daily chores.

Why do doctors recommend exercise for depression?

When you exercise, your body releases chemicals called endorphins. These endorphins interact with the receptors in your brain that reduce your perception of pain to make it easier so that you can exercise for longer. Endorphins also trigger a positive feeling in the body, similar to that of morphine. This is the reason why while suffering from depressing many doctors recommend exercise as one of the solutions.

Benefits of exercise

Improves health and mood — During exercise, your body releases endorphins that improve your mood and make you feel more relaxed

Reduces risk of heart diseases — Exercise strengthens your heart and improves your circulation. The increased blood flow raises the oxygen levels in your body. This helps lower your risk of heart diseases such as high cholesterol, coronary artery disease, and heart attack. Regular exercise can also lower your blood pressure.

Help your body manage blood sugar and insulin levels — Exercise can lower your blood sugar level and help your insulin work better. This can cut down your risk for metabolic syndrome and type 2 diabetes

Help keep your thinking, learning, and judgment skills sharp as you age — Exercise stimulates your body to release proteins and other chemicals that improve the structure and function of your brain.

Reduce your risk of some cancers — including colon, breast, uterus and lung cancer.

Improves sleep — exercise can help you sleep better and quicker.

Strengthens the bones — helps strengthen bones of children and teenagers.
