Books, our legacy

My great great grandfather was a very learned man and a big books enthusiast. Due to his fondness of reading , we have many books from the early nineteenth and late eighteenth century. We really cherish those books. These are a part of our family legacy. Some of the fascinating books and there publishing dates are:

William Shakespeare the whole series.

William Shakespeare was an English playwright, poet, and actor, widely regarded as the greatest writer in the English language and the world’s greatest dramatist. He is often called England’s national poet and the “Bard of Avon”

Publishing year 1852

Mr. Chamberlain

Year of publishing 1904

Neville Chamberlain was the prime minister of the United Kingdom from 1937 to 1940. He is best known for his role in the Munich Agreement of 1938 which ceded parts of Czechoslovakia to Hitler and is now the most popular example of the foreign policy known as appeasement. This book tells about his life and career

Queen Victoria, her life and reign

Year of publishing 1901

Victoria was Queen of the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland from 20 June 1837 until her death in 1901. Known as the Victorian era, her reign of 63 years and seven months was longer than any previous British monarch

The world of Today

A survey of the lands and peoples of the globe as seen in travel and commerce in 1912. Volume 3 looks at the Nile Lands, Abyssinia and it Borderlands, the Barbary States, the Sahara, the Soudan region, Central Africa, South Africa and then has the geographical and commercial survey.

year of publishing 1905

Complete work of Oliver goldsmith

Oliver Goldsmith (10 November 1728–4 April 1774) was an Anglo-Irish novelist, playwright and poet, who is best known for his novel The Vicar of Wakefield (1766), his pastoral poem The Deserted Village (1770), and his plays The Good-Natured Man (1768) and She Stoops to Conquer (1771, first performed in 1773).

year of publishing 1888

The Webster dictionary

In 1828, George and Charles Merriam founded the company as G & C Merriam Co. in Springfield, Massachusetts

year of publishing 1891

The Byron’s life and letters

The letters of Lord Byron, of which about 3,000 are known, range in date from 1798, when Byron was 10 years old, to 9 April 1824, a few days before he died. They have long received extraordinary critical praise for their wit, spontaneity and sincerity.

year of publishing 1892

We as a family are avid readers too and hope to expand the collection of books and pass it on from generation to generation.
