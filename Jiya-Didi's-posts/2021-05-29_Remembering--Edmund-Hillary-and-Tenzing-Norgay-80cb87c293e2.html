Remembering, Edmund Hillary and Tenzing Norgay

Climbing mount Everest

Edmund Hillary, a mountain climber from new Zealand, accompanied by a Tenzing Norgay from Tibet were the first to reach the summit of mount Everest. They accomplished this feat on 29th may 1953. It is said that they reached the peak around 11:30 in the morning and they stayed at the peak for 15 minutes.

Edmund Hillary was born on 20 July 1919, a budding mountaineer and Antarctic explorer he was interested in mountaineering from an early age. At the age of 33 Edmund Hillary, accompanied by Tenzing Norgay reached the summit of mount Everest which was led by John Hunt.

Tensing Norgay, born on May 15th 1914 was the son of a mountaineer and guide. Due to his father’s interest Tensing also inherited a budding interest in mountaineering. At the small age of 17 Tensing reached the summit of the world’s highest peak. A British mountaineer Eric Shipton suggested his name for the expedition.
