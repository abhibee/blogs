Story of writing a story

“I am an excellent writer", this was the thought in my mind when I entered into the 30 days blogathon started by my brother “ and I can write anything anytime, not a big deal". But then, when I actually sat with my laptop that the reality strike and I kept on thinking for hours at a stretch on what to write. Many wonderful ideas came to my mind but the next moment the same mind refused to tell me more about the same. I felt betrayed by my intelligent but snooty mind. I depended on it so much when I agreed to jump into the writing pool but now it looked like a deep well and I felt as if I was about to drown if it won’t support me.

It suddenly strike me if I bribe my mind with the food it loves it may start working and so I googled about which food helps the mind to work fine and, as per Google’s recommendations, I ate lot of almonds and drank milk. I ate so much that my tummy started aching but my mind refused to buzz.

I started loosing my patience. Since the carrot was not working I decided to use the stick. I banged my head on the wall in order to punish the mind. Ouch!!! I got a bump on my head but the stubborn mind was still not listening.

I was exhausted now thinking what shall I do next so that my mind starts cooperating with me and help me write wonderful stories. Why my own mind, the one which has been with me through out my life is not helping me here. Suddenly I heard a voice coming from my mind.

The mind said that it never betrayed me instead it wanted me to understand that regardless of the wonderful ideas it can give, to make the stories fabulous it should always come through the heart. Now I understand the secret of writing beautiful stories is that it should be heartfelt. The ideas need to connect with the heart and the story should flow through it. If it does not appeal to me it won’t appeal to anyone.

You’ll never find ‘piece’ of mind until you listen to your heart.

By Reema Sharma on May 20, 2021.
